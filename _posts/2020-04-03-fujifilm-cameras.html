Overview


The challenge

Photographic cameras have always had a very similar design, even after they became digital (DSLR and more recently mirrorless) they still have similar looks and controls .

As an amateur photographer I’ve felt how difficult is to learn how to use a camera properly because it has a lot of buttons and features. Most recent Fujifilm's cameras have an interesting experience that is why I chosed this maker as a starting point.


My role and length of project

I have done this redesign exercise on my own and it took me about 4 months.

Process

Double diamond method was used on this project development.


Discovering

User research

I talked to few photographers and I found a very userful video (where two photographers were questioning current cameras designs) then I created a survey based on my notes about the video and my notes from the chats with photographers.


Fstoppers Youtube channel - video about cameras design


The main points presented on the survey were:

1 - Quicker and more intuitive way to change Aperture, ISO and shutter speed;

2 - Modes A, S, M aren’t most adequate. Let’s say you want to adjust Aperture and ISO, or shutter speed and aperture but you do not want a full manual mode;

3 - Dedicated White balance and Tint control with fast access;

4 - Shoot modes (single shot, burst), maybe a pressure sensor to change FPS while shooting;

5 - Easier and quicker way to move the focus point on screen;

6 - Easier an quicker way to zoom in and out on preview mode;

7 - Being able to save settings for photo or video and alternate between them quickly;

8 - When looking through the viewfinder (on mirrorless cameras), you should be able to see how the picture is exposed or without taking it into account;

9 - All (or most) buttons and adjusts accessible for the right hand, leaving the left hand with only one job (hold the camera);

10 -Screen - better quality, bigger and more responsible touch screen;

11 - Battery door on the side and not on the bottom;

12 - Better strap conection and easy lock/unlock system so you can exchange them easily and fast;

13 - Proper eyepiece locking system, to prevent losing it;

14 - Improving camera ergonomics;


7 photographers answered the survey.

Defining

An affinity map was created with the answers gathered from the survey.

Affinity map


As those points reach many different aspects of the camera and its use, and the users who answered the survey aren't exclusive fujifilm users, it was necessary to prioritize (see more on the bottom section "my learnings") to make it possible to define the design problems with a practical approach.



Design problems

1 - Lack of viewfinder customization: Be able to choose which information you want to see, be able to make modes changes (see that information on viewfinder) and be able to choose seeing a scene regard exposition/regardless exposition;

2 - Touch screen: If always sensitive it creates a problem about accidental touches and yet it's a powerful tool which has not been widely used;

3 - Settings: In some cases photographers need changing back and forth between different light conditions or between photo and video so they need a way to save and quickly access sets of settings;

4 - Buttons/dials: Some are used a lot, others not so much but they are there to confuse new users or just ruins the camera's look. Usually there are no flexibility about what a certain button/dial does and users crave for customization;

5 - Zoom in/out and moving the focuss point aren't so practical.


NOTE: There are points related to some problems on the list that were solved in the newest fujifilm mirrorless models, but as those problems emerged from the research I should at least metion them before I prioritize.

My learnings

At some point I've noticed that I was going too broad on project's scope that I was more on a conceptual industrial design path than UX design. I had to prioritize and remember that UX is as much about technical possibilities and bussiness needs as the user's needs.

So I've realized that it is kind of easy for me to diverge and have new ideas but I gotta pay attention on the convergent stages because they are so important as having ideas.
