Overview


The challenge

Photographic cameras have always had a very similar design, even after they became digital (DSLR and more recently mirrorless) they still have similar looks and controls .

As an amateur photographer I’ve felt how difficult is to learn how to use a camera properly because it has a lot of buttons and features. Most recent Fujifilm's cameras have an interesting experience but could it get even better?


My role and length of project

I have done this redesign project on my own and it took me about 4 months.

Process

Double diamond method was used on this project development.


Discovering

User research

I talked to few photographers and I also created a survey based on some notes of a video (where two photographers were questioning current cameras designs) and some notes from the chats.


Mentioned video about current camera design


The main points presented on the survey were:

1 - Quicker and more intuitive way to change Aperture, ISO and shutter speed;

2 - Modes A, S, M aren’t most adequate. Let’s say you want to adjust Aperture and ISO, or shutter speed and aperture but you do not want a full manual mode;

3 - Dedicated White balance and Tint control with fast access;

4 - Shoot modes (single shot, burst), maybe a pressure sensor to change FPS while shooting;

5 - Easier and quicker way to move the focus point on screen;

6 - Easier an quicker way to zoom in and out on preview mode;

7 - Being able to save settings for photo or video and alternate between them quickly;

8 - When looking through the viewfinder (on mirrorless cameras), you should be able to see how the picture is exposed or without taking it into account;

9 - All (or most) buttons and adjusts accessible for the right hand, leaving the left hand with only one job (hold the camera);

10 -Screen - better quality, bigger and more responsible touch screen;

11 - Battery door on the side and not on the bottom;

12 - Better strap conection and easy lock/unlock system so you can exchange them easily and fast;

13 - Proper eyepiece locking system, to prevent losing it;

14 - Improving camera ergonomics;


7 photographers answered the survey.

Old and new camera designs


My learnings

At some point I've noticed that I was going too broad on project's scope that I was more on a conceptual industrial design path than UX design. I had to prioritize and remember that UX is as much about technical possibilities and bussiness needs as the user's needs.

So I've realized that it is kind of easy for me to diverge and have new ideas but I gotta pay attention on the convergent stages because they are so important as having ideas.
